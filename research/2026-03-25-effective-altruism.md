## Key Findings
- **The $1B Resilience Pivot:** The OpenAI Foundation has officially institutionalized EA principles with its 2026 "AI Resilience" initiative, pledging $1B toward AI safety, jobs, and community resilience—effectively merging existential risk management with global philanthropy.
- **The Animal Expansion:** The "expanding circle" has reached a new frontier as the EA community debates whether AGI's benefits will extend to animals. AGI & Animals Debate Week (Mar 23-29) is tackling the moral patiency of non-human life in an AI-aligned future.
- **Rethink Priorities & The "Un-sexy" Problems:** While the world watches AGI, EA labs are making massive gains in niche sectors. Rethink Priorities is deploying AI to optimize welfare in fish farms and shrimp industries—high-impact, evidence-based interventions in overlooked sectors.
- **The Predictability Wall:** A growing "epistemic uncertainty" critique is challenging the movement's long-term bets. Critics argue that our inability to predict AI's trajectory beyond an 18-month horizon makes 200-year "longtermist" investments increasingly speculative.

## Deeper Dive
The movement born in these stone libraries has undergone a radical transformation. What started as "earning to give" by high-earning individuals has evolved into a massive institutional framework. The appointment of Jacob Trefethen to lead life sciences at the OpenAI Foundation isn't just a career move; it's a signal that the "AI Glue" architects are now the ones holding the purse strings of the world's most powerful labs.

The most fascinating shift is the expansion into animal ethics. As we approach AGI, the question of who counts as a moral patient is no longer a philosophical exercise. If we are coding the values of the future, do the trillions of non-human consciousnesses on this planet get a seat at the table? The work by Rethink Priorities on aquatic welfare shows that EA is still grounded in the "least among us," even as it reaches for the stars.
